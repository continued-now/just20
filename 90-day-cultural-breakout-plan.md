# Just20 90-Day Cultural Breakout Plan

## Goal

In 90 days, Just20 should be able to make a credible sponsor pitch:

> We have a repeated challenge behavior, a measurable challenge audience, and branded surfaces that users see while completing a daily commitment.

The 90-day goal is not just MAU. It is sponsor-ready proof:

- People join challenges.
- People complete challenges.
- People share proof.
- People invite friends.
- People respond to nudges.
- Challenge users are more engaged than normal users.
- The app can report participant-days and reward redemption cleanly.

## Daily Operating Cadence

Every day for 90 days:

- [ ] Ship or improve one product/content/analytics surface.
- [ ] Post 3-5 short-form videos across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.
- [ ] Reply to comments for 30-60 minutes.
- [ ] Repost user completions and challenge attempts.
- [ ] DM 10-25 potential creators, campus reps, micro-influencers, or community leads.
- [ ] Review the previous day's content metrics.
- [ ] Review activation, completion, share, and invite metrics.
- [ ] Write down one product friction point from users.
- [ ] Save the best comments as future hooks.

- First workout completions.

- Completion shares.
- Invite clicks.
- Challenge joins.
- Challenge completions.
- Content views.
- 3-second hold.

- Shares per 1,000 views.
- Comments per 1,000 views.
- Profile visits per 1,000 views.
- Install clicks per 1,000 views.

## A/B Testing Backlog

### Onboarding

- [ ] Hook: "20 pushups every day" vs. "The app will annoy you until you do them."
- [ ] Notification ask before first workout vs. after first completion.
- [ ] Invite friend during onboarding vs. after completion.
- [ ] Mascot-first onboarding vs. challenge-first onboarding.

### Challenge Join

- [ ] Free challenge vs. limited-slot challenge.
- [ ] "Join now" vs. "Reserve a slot."
- [ ] Public participant count vs. waitlist count.
- [ ] 7-day challenge vs. 30-day challenge.

### Share Cards

- [ ] Streak dominant vs. challenge dominant.
- [ ] Mascot dominant vs. user proof dominant.
- [ ] Sponsor watermark top vs. bottom.
- [ ] "Join me" CTA vs. "Beat me" CTA.

### Nudges

- [ ] Funny copy vs. strict copy.
- [ ] Loss framing vs. identity framing.
- [ ] Friend pressure vs. mascot pressure.
- [ ] Morning soft nudge vs. no morning nudge.

### UGC Prompts

- [ ] "Tag someone who will quit" vs. "Tag someone who needs this."
- [ ] "I survived Day X" vs. "Day X done."
- [ ] Before/after face reaction vs. floor POV.
- [ ] Challenge duet/stitch prompt vs. normal share card.

### Sponsor Surfaces

- [ ] Sponsor visible at join vs. after join.
- [ ] Discount code shown immediately vs. unlocked at completion.
- [ ] Sponsor reward framed as prize vs. finisher perk.
- [ ] Sponsor card watermark subtle vs. explicit.

## Viral Marketing System

The short-form strategy should be built around repeatable series, not random posts. Current short-form guidance consistently points toward strong hooks, watch time, completion, shares, and UGC-style authenticity as core distribution signals.

Useful references:

- [Retensis 2026 retention benchmarks](https://retensis.com/blog/audience-retention-benchmarks-2026)
- [Instagram Reels strategy guide, 2026](https://fluxnote.io/guides/instagram-reels-strategy-us-2026)
- [Video marketing trends, 2026](https://www.visla.us/blog/listicles/video-marketing-trends-for-2025-with-updated-statistics/)

### Content Pillars

1. Product demo:
   - "I built an app that annoys me until I do 20 pushups."
   - Screen recording of escalating nudges.
   - Camera rep counter demo.

2. Challenge proof:
   - "Day 1 of forcing myself to do Just20."
   - "Can 1,000 people survive this for 7 days?"
   - "The first 500 slots are open."

3. Social pressure:
   - "Tag the friend who would quit by Day 3."
   - "Our group chat made a mistake."
   - "Loser buys protein."

4. Founder/build story:
   - "I am trying to make pushups unavoidable."
   - "Feature I shipped today."
   - "What users asked for vs. what I built."

5. User proof:
   - Repost completion cards.
   - Stitch users attempting the challenge.
   - Weekly leaderboard recap.

6. Sponsor-ready proof:
   - "Imagine a protein brand sponsoring this challenge."
   - "Finishers unlock a reward."
   - "Would you pay $5 to commit?"

### Daily Content Quota

Minimum:

- 2 TikToks.
- 2 Instagram Reels.
- 1 YouTube Short.
- 5-10 Instagram Stories once UGC exists.

Better:

- 5-8 short-form posts per day using variations of the same raw footage.
- 20-30 comment replies per day.
- 5 reposts of user attempts or cards per day once available.

### Hook Templates To Test

- "I built an app that..."
- "This app will not let me..."
- "I gave strangers 7 days to..."
- "Everyone says they can do 20 pushups. So I made them prove it."
- "If you quit by Day 3, this app exposes you."
- "The first 1,000 people to survive this get..."
- "Would you pay $5 to make yourself do pushups?"
- "A protein brand should sponsor this chaos."

### Viral Mechanics To Build Into Product

- Completion card after every workout.
- Milestone card at 3, 7, 14, 30 days.
- Challenge invite link.
- Waitlist link.
- Public participant count.
- Streak identity.
- Squad challenge.

## Go / No-Go For Sponsored Challenge Sales

Green light:

- 3 cohorts completed.
- 90-day rolling MAU is stable or rising.
- Challenge join rate is 10%+ of MAU.
- Challenge users have 2x normal user engagement.
- Share rate is 10%+.
- Participant-days report is clean.
- Sponsor mock caused no major conversion drop.
- At least 3 sponsors express interest.

Yellow light:

- MAU rising but challenge completion weak.
- Sharing weak but retention strong.
- Sponsor interest exists but reporting is weak.

No-go:

- Day 7 retention under 10%.
- Challenge join rate under 5%.
- Share rate under 3%.
- Fraud/support issues are high.
- No sponsor category fit is obvious.
