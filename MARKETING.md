# Just20 Marketing Plan

## Purpose

This document turns `developmentplan.md` and `MONETIZATION.md` into a concrete marketing operating plan. The goal is to use viral short-form content, social proof, and challenge loops to grow Just20 while building toward a profitable subscription and paid add-on business.

Just20 should market itself as a simple, funny, slightly brutal accountability app:

> 20 pushups. Every day. The app will annoy you until you do them.

The core marketing engine is not paid ads first. It is user-generated proof: completion cards, streak milestones, buddy challenges, squad streaks, and creator-led challenges.

## Marketing Strategy

### Main Bet

Just20 grows when users publicly prove they did the work and challenge friends to do the same.

The product should make every completed workout create a natural marketing asset:

- A shareable completion card.
- A streak identity marker: `DAY 7`, `DAY 30`, `DAY 100`.
- A friend invite code.
- A challenge prompt.
- A reason to tag someone.

### Core Funnel

```
Short-form video or shared card
  -> app install
  -> onboarding
  -> notification permission
  -> first workout
  -> completion card share
  -> friend invite
  -> buddy streak or challenge
  -> 7-day streak
  -> Pro upsell
```

### Positioning

Just20 is not a workout library, gym tracker, or generic habit app.

It is:

- A daily accountability game.
- A pushup streak challenge.
- A social pressure app.
- A shareable proof-of-work ritual.
- A funny anti-excuse brand.

### Target Users

Primary:

- Men and women ages 16-35 who use Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, or Snapchat.
- People who like simple fitness challenges.
- People who respond to streaks, public accountability, and humor.
- Students, young professionals, friend groups, office teams, and creators.

Secondary:

- Fitness beginners who want a low-friction daily habit.
- Creators who need simple audience challenges.
- Small communities that want a shared daily ritual.

## Brand Voice

The voice should be direct, funny, and lightly aggressive. It should feel like a friend calling you out, not a corporation selling fitness.

Use:

- Short sentences.
- Plain language.
- Challenge-based copy.
- Streak identity.
- Social pressure.
- Humor.

Avoid:

- Medical claims.
- Body-shaming.
- Overly cruel copy.
- Complicated fitness language.
- Generic motivational quotes as the main hook.

Example lines:

- "The floor is waiting."
- "20 pushups. No negotiation."
- "Your streak dies tonight."
- "Do it before the app gets worse."
- "Tag the friend who will quit first."
- "Day 7 is where excuses start getting loud."

## Channel Strategy

### Instagram Reels

Primary channel for launch.

Use Instagram for:

- Reels demonstrating the app.
- Story reposts of completion cards.
- Daily challenge prompts.
- Creator collabs.
- Polls and Q&A.
- Highlight reels for streak milestones.

Recommended cadence:

- 2 Reels per day for the first 30 days.
- 5-10 Stories per day once users are sharing cards.
- Daily reposts of user completion cards.
- Weekly challenge announcement every Sunday.

### TikTok

Use TikTok for top-of-funnel experiments.

Recommended cadence:

- 2-3 TikToks per day for the first 30 days.
- Test many hooks quickly.
- Repost winning TikToks to Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts.

### YouTube Shorts

Use Shorts as a secondary distribution channel.

Recommended cadence:

- 1-2 Shorts per day.
- Reuse the best TikTok/Reels content.
- Build search-friendly titles like "I built an app that forces you to do 20 pushups."

### Reddit and Communities

Use carefully. Do not spam.

Good communities:

- Fitness challenge communities.
- Habit-building communities.
- Indie app founder communities.
- College/community Discords.
- Productivity groups.

Approach:

- Share the build story.
- Ask for testers.
- Offer a 7-day challenge.
- Avoid posting only download links.

### Creator Outreach

Creators are useful because the app is a challenge format.

Target:

- Micro fitness creators: 5k-100k followers.
- College lifestyle creators.
- Comedy creators who do physical challenges.
- Productivity/self-improvement creators.
- Military/prep/discipline creators, if brand fit is good.

Offer:

- Custom invite code.
- Featured challenge.
- Free Pro.
- Creator leaderboard.
- Revenue share later, once payment exists.

KPIs:

## Content Formats to Test

### Format A: Fast Demo

Structure:

1. Hook in first 1 second.
2. Show app notification.
3. Show user doing pushups.
4. Show counter hitting 20.
5. Show completion card.
6. CTA: "Try the 7-day challenge."

Example hook:

> I made an app that bullies me into doing pushups.

### Format B: Friend Challenge

Structure:

1. Hook: "Send this to the friend who says they are getting fit."
2. Explain: "20 pushups a day. If one of you misses, the streak dies."
3. Show buddy invite.
4. CTA: "Start a buddy streak."

### Format C: Streak Drama

Structure:

1. Hook: "My 13-day streak almost died."
2. Show 9pm notification.
3. Show pushups.
4. Show streak saved.
5. CTA: "Do yours before midnight."

### Format D: Mascot Meme

Structure:

1. Hook: "The app after I ignore the first 12 reminders."
2. Show escalating mascot/notification copy.
3. End with "Fine. I did the pushups."

### Format E: Public Accountability

Structure:

1. Hook: "I will do 20 pushups every day until this app has 10,000 users."
2. Daily update.
3. Show streak.
4. Ask viewers to join.

## Viral Mechanics

### Must-Have Viral Loops

1. Completion card loop:
   - User completes workout.
   - App generates card.
   - User shares to Instagram/TikTok.
   - Viewer installs and starts challenge.

2. Buddy loop:
   - User invites friend.
   - Friend installs.
   - Both build shared streak.
   - Buddy milestone creates another share moment.

3. Challenge loop:
   - User creates named challenge.
   - Users invite friends.
   - Group leaderboard creates pressure.
   - Challenge results become shareable.

4. Milestone loop:
   - User hits Day 7/30/100.
   - App celebrates.
   - User shares milestone card.
   - Viewers ask how they got it.

### Share Prompts

Use prompts that create social pressure:

- "Post this or it did not happen."
- "Tag someone who would quit before Day 3."
- "Send this to your weakest friend."
- "Start a buddy streak."
- "Make the group chat prove it."

Keep some prompts softer:

- "Share your Day 1."
- "Invite a friend to keep you honest."
- "Build the streak together."

## Monetization-Aware Marketing

Marketing should first sell the habit, then sell the paid layer after users feel value.

### Free Marketing Promise

Use this publicly:

- "Do 20 pushups every day."
- "Track your streak."
- "Get bullied by notifications."
- "Share proof."
- "Invite a buddy."

### Pro Marketing Promise

Use this after the user has traction:

- "Make it harder to quit."
- "Build squad streaks."
- "Unlock premium cards."
- "Customize your nudge intensity."
- "Protect your streak."

### What Not to Lead With

Do not lead acquisition content with:

- Subscription pricing.
- Repair token purchases.
- Advanced stats.
- Backend/social complexity.

Lead with the challenge. Monetize commitment later.

## 12-Week Marketing Plan

### Week 1: Foundation and Tracking

Goal:

- Make sure marketing traffic can be measured and converted.

Actions:

- Create Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and X accounts.
- Lock handle, bio, profile image, and link-in-bio.
- Write the one-line positioning: "20 pushups. Every day. The app will annoy you until you do them."
- Set up landing page or app-store waitlist if stores are not ready.
- Add analytics events listed above.
- Create 20 short-form scripts.
- Create 10 notification/meme screenshots.
- Create 5 completion card mockups.
- Recruit first 20 testers from friends, fitness communities, and founder networks.

Content:

- 2 Reels/TikToks per day.
- 1 build-in-public post per day.
- 3 Stories per day.

KPIs:

- 20 testers recruited.
- 14 short-form posts shipped.
- 500 total views minimum.
- 20 waitlist signups or test installs.
- Analytics dashboard working.
- Baseline onboarding completion rate measured.

Decision:

- If viewers do not understand the product in comments, simplify the hook.

### Week 2: Hook Testing

Goal:

- Find the first content angles that generate comments, shares, and installs.

Actions:

- Test 5 hooks:
  - "I built an app that bullies me into doing pushups."
  - "This app gives you 20 random chances to stop being lazy."
  - "20 pushups a day. If you miss, your streak dies."
  - "Tag the friend who would quit first."
  - "I am doing 20 pushups every day until this app goes viral."
- Post each hook in at least 2 variants.
- DM every commenter who shows interest.
- Ask testers to post their Day 1 card.
- Create a public "7-Day Just20 Challenge" Story highlight.

Content:

- 2-3 Reels/TikToks per day.
- 1 Story repost per user share.
- 1 founder update per day.

KPIs:

- 20 posts shipped this week.
- 2 posts above 1,000 views.
- Average 3-second hold rate above 50%.
- Share rate above 0.5%.
- 50 waitlist signups or installs.
- 10 completion cards shared by testers.

Decision:

- Keep only the top 2 hooks for Week 3.

### Week 3: First Challenge Launch

Goal:

- Turn passive viewers into participants.

Actions:

- Launch "7 Days No Excuses" challenge.
- Ask users to post Day 1 completion card and tag the account.
- Create daily Story template:
  - "Day X. Did you do your 20?"
  - Poll: "Done / dodging it"
- DM 50 micro-creators with a simple challenge invite.
- Repost every user completion card.
- Track challenge participants manually if needed.

Content:

- 2 Reels/TikToks per day.
- Daily challenge update.
- Daily participant reposts.
- 1 creator outreach batch per day.

KPIs:

- 100 challenge signups or installs.
- 30 users complete Day 1.
- 15 users post/share completion proof.
- 5 creator replies.
- Day 1 retention above 30%.
- Notification opt-in above 50%.

Decision:

- If Day 1 completion is weak, improve onboarding and first-workout CTA before scaling.

### Week 4: Social Proof Week

Goal:

- Make the app feel alive and participatory.

Actions:

- Publish user proof compilations.
- Create "Wall of Shame / Wall of Streaks" Story series.
- Feature top streaks from testers.
- Ask users to tag one friend.
- Add stronger in-app copy around sharing completion cards if needed.
- Start a weekly public leaderboard manually on Instagram Stories.

Content:

- 2 Reels/TikToks per day.
- 5-10 Stories per day.
- 1 weekly leaderboard post.
- 1 carousel explaining how the challenge works.

KPIs:

- 25 user-generated shares this week.
- 100 invites sent or invite-code shares.
- 200 new installs/signups.
- 20% of active users share at least one card.
- Day 7 retention cohort target: 15%+.

Decision:

- If users do not share, improve card design and share prompt before paid growth.

### Week 5: Buddy Streak Push

Goal:

- Shift from solo habit to friend accountability.

Actions:

- Launch "Bring one friend" campaign.
- Post friend-challenge content daily.
- Add social copy to captions:
  - "Send this to the friend who always says Monday."
  - "One of you will quit first."
- DM active users asking them to invite one friend.
- Recruit friend pairs for testimonials.
- Start collecting buddy streak screenshots.

Content:

- 2 Reels/TikToks per day.
- 1 friend challenge Story poll per day.
- 1 testimonial or user proof per day.

KPIs:

- 30% of active users view invite code.
- 15% of active users share invite code.
- 50 buddy links created.
- 300 new installs/signups.
- K-factor baseline measured.

Decision:

- If invites are low, make invite prompt more prominent after completion.

### Week 6: Creator Micro-Campaign

Goal:

- Use micro-creators to test repeatable acquisition.

Actions:

- Outreach to 150 micro-creators.
- Offer custom challenge codes.
- Give creators a simple brief:
  - "Try Just20 for 7 days."
  - "Post Day 1 and Day 7."
  - "Challenge your audience."
- Offer free Pro later when subscriptions exist.
- Track each creator with a unique code or link.

Content:

- 2 owned posts per day.
- Creator stitches/duets/reposts.
- Story highlight for creator challenges.

KPIs:

- 150 creator DMs.
- 15 creator replies.
- 5 creators post.
- 500 installs/signups from creator traffic.
- Creator install cost below expected paid ad CPI.
- 25% first-workout completion from creator users.

Decision:

- Double down on creator categories with the best install-to-workout rate.

### Week 7: Milestone and Identity Push

Goal:

- Make streak identity the reason users continue and share.

Actions:

- Promote Day 7, Day 14, Day 30 milestone cards.
- Post "Day 7 club" compilations.
- Add weekly milestone shoutouts.
- Create content around streak repair and freeze tokens.
- Begin teasing Pro as "for people who are actually serious."

Content:

- 2 Reels/TikToks per day.
- Daily milestone reposts.
- 1 carousel: "What happens after Day 7?"

KPIs:

- 20% of users reach Day 7 in current cohort.
- 30 milestone card shares.
- 10% profile-to-install conversion.
- 5% paywall interest if paywall preview exists.
- Day 7 retention above 18%.

Decision:

- If milestones are not shared, make the card more dramatic and identity-focused.

### Week 8: Soft Monetization Test

Goal:

- Learn whether users will pay without damaging virality.

Actions:

- Add or preview Pro waitlist.
- Test Pro messaging:
  - Premium cards.
  - Squad streaks.
  - Nudge controls.
  - Streak protection.
- Ask active users which Pro feature they would pay for.
- Create a "Founding Pro" waitlist.
- Keep basic sharing free.

Content:

- 1-2 Pro teaser posts.
- 2 normal viral posts per day.
- 1 founder poll per day.

KPIs:

- 5% of active users join Pro waitlist.
- 100 Pro waitlist signups.
- No drop in completion card share rate.
- 20 qualitative responses on paid features.
- Top paid feature identified.

Decision:

- If users reject repair-token monetization, lead with premium cards and squads instead.

### Week 9: Challenge Template Week

Goal:

- Turn the app into a repeatable group challenge format.

Actions:

- Launch named challenge templates:
  - 7 Days No Excuses.
  - 30-Day Morning Push.
  - Squad vs. Squad Week.
- Create challenge-specific share assets.
- Recruit 10 groups to run private challenges.
- Post results and group screenshots.

Content:

- 2 Reels/TikToks per day.
- Daily challenge leaderboard Story.
- 1 challenge explainer carousel.

KPIs:

- 10 group challenges started.
- 100 challenge participants.
- 25% challenge participant Day 3 retention.
- 15% of participants invite at least one person.
- 500 new installs/signups.

Decision:

- If group retention beats solo retention, prioritize squads and challenge backend.

### Week 10: Paid Conversion Prep

Goal:

- Prepare users to understand Pro before launch.

Actions:

- Publish "what should be free forever?" poll.
- Publish "what should Pro include?" poll.
- Build early supporter list.
- Offer founding price concept:
  - $29.99/year for early supporters.
- Create Pro feature landing copy.
- Prepare app-store screenshots around free core and Pro upgrades.

Content:

- 1 Pro education post every other day.
- Continue 2 viral posts per day.
- Daily user proof Stories.

KPIs:

- 200 founding Pro waitlist users.
- 10% active-user paywall click rate if in app.
- 3% stated purchase intent from active users.
- No decline in Day 1 completion rate.

Decision:

- If purchase intent is below 2%, delay monetization and keep improving retention.

### Week 11: Pro or Founding Supporter Launch

Goal:

- Start earning revenue while preserving free virality.

Actions:

- Launch Pro if in-app purchase is ready, or launch founding supporter waitlist if not.
- Lead with:
  - Premium share cards.
  - Squad streaks.
  - Nudge controls.
  - Advanced stats.
- Offer annual founding price.
- Give active Day 7+ users the first offer.
- Avoid showing paywall before first completed workout.

Content:

- 1 launch announcement.
- 2 viral posts per day.
- 1 founder explanation post.
- User proof continues.

KPIs:

- 2-5% active-user free-to-paid conversion.
- 20 paid users or founding commitments.
- Trial start rate above 5% from eligible users.
- Refund/complaint rate below 2%.
- Completion card share rate unchanged.

Decision:

- If paywall hurts sharing, move premium card upsell later and keep share flow cleaner.

### Week 12: Review and Scale

Goal:

- Decide what to scale and what to cut.

Actions:

- Review content performance by hook, format, and channel.
- Review acquisition funnel:
  - View -> profile -> install -> onboarding -> workout -> share -> invite.
- Review retention cohorts.
- Review creator performance.
- Review revenue and conversion.
- Pick next 4-week sprint:
  - More creator outreach.
  - More challenge templates.
  - Pro card packs.
  - Squad backend.
  - Paid ads test.

Content:

- Recap post: "What happened after 12 weeks of forcing people to do pushups."
- Best user proof compilation.
- Next challenge announcement.

KPIs:

- Clear winning content format.
- Day 1 retention above 35%.
- Day 7 retention above 20%.
- Completion card share rate above 15%.
- Invite rate above 0.3 invites per active user.
- Free-to-paid conversion above 2% if Pro launched.
- Backend cost per MAU known and acceptable.

Decision:

- Scale only the channel and hook with measurable installs and retained users.

## Content Scorecard

Grade every post after 48 hours.

| Metric | Weak | Good | Strong |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 3-second hold | <35% | 35-55% | >55% |
| Average watch time | <25% | 25-50% | >50% |
| Share rate | <0.3% | 0.3-1% | >1% |
| Comment rate | <0.5% | 0.5-2% | >2% |
| Profile visits per 1k views | <5 | 5-20 | >20 |
| Installs per 1k views | <1 | 1-5 | >5 |

Rules:

- Kill hooks that fail 3 times.
- Remake posts with high watch time but low installs using a clearer CTA.
- Remake posts with high comments but low watch time using a faster opening.
- Turn posts with high shares into series.

## Paid Ads Later

Do not start with paid ads unless organic hooks already convert.

Only test paid ads after:

- Day 1 retention is above 30%.
- First-workout completion is above 50%.
- Completion card share rate is above 10%.
- At least one organic video has exceeded 10,000 views.
- App-store page conversion is measurable.

Initial paid test:

- Budget: $20-$50/day for 7 days.
- Promote the best organic post, not a polished generic ad.
- Optimize for installs only after activation is healthy.

KPIs:

- CPI below expected LTV.
- Install-to-first-workout above 40%.
- Day 1 retention above 25% from paid users.
- No major drop in notification permission opt-in.

## First 30 Days Summary

The first month is not about monetization first. It is about proving the viral loop.

Minimum success by Day 30:

- 60 short-form posts shipped.
- 500 installs or waitlist signups.
- 100 first workouts completed.
- 25 completion cards shared.
- 50 invite-code shares.
- 10 buddy pairs or challenge groups.
- Day 1 retention above 30%.
- Day 7 retention above 15%.
- One repeatable content format identified.

If these numbers are not close, fix activation and share mechanics before building more paid features.

## 90-Day Targets

Product:

- Day 1 retention: 40%.
- Day 7 retention: 22%.
- Day 30 retention: 18%.
- Completion card share rate: 15-25%.
- Notification opt-in: 60%+.

Growth:

- 10,000 installs or waitlist signups.
- 1,000 weekly active users.
- 0.3+ invites per active user.

Revenue:

## Final Rule

Every marketing action should answer one of these questions:

- Does this make someone want to try Day 1?
- Does this make a user share proof?
- Does this make a user invite a friend?
- Does this make a streak feel worth protecting?
- Does this make Pro feel like stronger accountability, not a tax?

If a tactic does none of those, cut it.
