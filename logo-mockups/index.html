Just 20 brand sprint

A logo that feels like a tiny button you want to press.

These are vector-first directions designed to work as a landing wordmark, app header, app icon, and favicon. The tiny previews are intentionally brutal: if it works there, it works anywhere.

Recommended path

Option A as the core mark, Option B as the full wordmark.

Option A has the cleanest favicon behavior because the `20` lives inside a Mac-like active toggle. Option B keeps the friendly product name visible on landing pages while letting the same green `20` control detach into an icon.

A Best favicon

Action Toggle

Feels like turning the daily promise on: `just` is the mode, `20` is the knob you press.

Use as app icon, favicon, notification icon, CTA badge. Keep the rep line subtle.

B Best wordmark

Word + Button

The clearest landing-page identity: Just is the promise, `20` is the action.

Use as navbar logo, app header, social profile banner.

C Most mascot-like

Happy Counter

Very reward-app friendly. The risk is that yellow mascot energy can drift away from fitness.

Use if the brand leans harder into pet/streak companion.

D Most fitness-coded

Rep Floor

Signals body tracking and pushups most directly, but it is less cute and weaker as a favicon.

Use as a secondary workout-mode icon, not the main mark.

E Most badge-like

Streak Shield

Good for streak protection and no-excuses energy. A little more serious than the current landing tone.

Use if streak defense becomes the dominant brand story.

F Most minimal

Minimal Rep Line

Cleanest and most grown-up. Strong on landing, but not as distinctive in app-icon form.

Use if the product needs to feel more premium and less toy-like.

Implementation notes

Primary lockup

Use the modified Option A active-toggle mark for landing nav, onboarding, app headers, and splash.

Icon system

Use Option A's compact toggle favicon anywhere the full wordmark gets too small.

Avoid

Do not use tiny pushup silhouettes as the favicon. They lose meaning under `32px` and feel less ownable.
