# Just20 Moonshot Revenue Model

## Assumption

This model assumes Just20 reaches 1,000,000 monthly active users globally and can reliably fill region-specific sponsored challenge blocks.

It also assumes Just20 has already proven:

- Strong challenge participation.
- Reliable daily engagement.
- Low fraud.
- Clean payment and rebate operations.
- Sponsor reporting.
- Sponsor code redemption.
- Repeatable challenge launches.

This is not an MVP plan. This is the upside case if Just20 becomes a real global habit challenge platform.

## Core Concept

Just20 sells regional category-exclusive challenge sponsorships.

Instead of one global protein sponsor blocking the whole app, Just20 can sell:

- USA protein exclusive.
- Korea protein exclusive.
- Japan protein exclusive.
- UK/EU protein exclusive.
- Regional hydration exclusives.
- Regional apparel/gym/wearable exclusives.

This lets domestic brands buy the region they actually care about while keeping global inventory valuable.

Example:

- Korea has 100,000 MAU.
- USA has 500,000 MAU.
- Rest of world has 400,000 MAU.
- A Korean protein drink company can sponsor only Korea.
- A USA supplement brand can sponsor only the USA.
- Both can be category-exclusive in their own markets.

## Challenge Economics

Working assumptions:

| Metric | Assumption |
|---|---:|
| User commitment fee | $5 |
| Completion rebate | $3 |
| Completion rate | 60% |
| Payment/support reserve | $0.60 per entrant |
| Net commitment margin | ~$2.60 per entrant |
| Daily challenge engagement | ~65% |
| Sponsor pricing basis | $0.08-$0.25 per branded participant-day |

Participant-day means one user actively participating in a branded challenge on one day.

The sponsor sales argument should be:

> Your brand was attached to verified workout-days, not passive impressions.

## 3-Month Regional Category Exclusive Pricing

| Region / Scale | 3-Month Category Exclusive |
|---|---:|
| Korea, 100,000 MAU | $50,000-$200,000 |
| USA, 500,000 MAU | $250,000-$1,000,000+ |

Do not sell global exclusivity cheaply. Regional exclusivity creates more inventory and lets local brands pay for what they can actually activate.

## Inventory Model

At 1M global MAU, Just20 could run regional category blocks.

Recommended sponsored categories per major region:

- Protein / nutrition.
- Hydration.
- Apparel / gym / wearable.

Recommended concurrent sponsored blocks:

| Region Type | Sponsored Categories | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Major region, such as USA | 3-5 | Larger budgets and more slots |
| Strong local region, such as Korea | 2-3 | Domestic brand exclusivity |
| Smaller region | 1-2 | Keep scarcity and sales focus |

Keep one open paid commitment challenge and one free community challenge available in each major region for overflow and growth.

- Free community challenge.

Korea:

- Protein drink sponsor.

Japan / UK / EU / Southeast Asia:

- Add only when regional MAU and sponsor demand justify it.

## What Makes Sponsors Pay More

Sponsor pricing can rise when Just20 can prove:

- Challenge slots fill quickly.
- Waitlists exist.
- Users pay to commit.
- Users open the app daily during the challenge.
- Completion cards are shared.
- Sponsor discount codes are redeemed.
- Sponsor landing pages receive traffic.
- Challenge participants retain better than normal users.
- Sponsor renewal performance is measurable.

Best headline metric:

> Your brand was attached to 1.5M verified workout-days this quarter in the USA.

## Key Risks

- Sponsor inventory does not fill.
- Challenge users churn after one campaign.
- Payment/rebate support becomes expensive.
- Fraud increases once rewards matter.
- Sponsor rewards are weak and users stop caring.
- App Store or payment rules limit the model.
- Regional legal requirements differ.
- Sponsor sales cycle is slower than expected.

## Operating Principle

The business should not sell generic ad inventory.

It should sell:

- Regional exclusivity.
- Daily engaged challenge attention.
- Verified workout-days.
- Scarce challenge slots.
- Sponsor reward redemption.
- Cultural association with discipline and consistency.

If Just20 gets to 1M MAU and the challenge loop works, this can become a multi-million-dollar annual sponsorship business, with upside into eight figures if multiple regions and categories fill consistently.
